About Me

I am a materials scientist and researcher working at the intersection of nanotechnology, thin-film engineering, and semiconductor processing. My work focuses on the deposition and characterization of functional thin films using Atomic Layer Deposition (ALD) and sputtering, with particular emphasis on ruthenium and ruthenium oxide systems for energy and device-related applications.

I use advanced characterization techniques such as XPS, XRD, AFM, Raman spectroscopy, ellipsometry, and electrochemical measurements to understand how processing conditions affect structure, surface chemistry, and performance. My broader interests include semiconductor manufacturing, metrology, process optimization, and scientific data analysis using Python.

Featured Research

ALD Ru to RuO₂ Phase Transformation

Investigated how annealing transforms ALD-deposited Ru metal into RuO₂ and how these changes influence surface chemistry, structure, and water adsorption behavior.

Reactive Sputtered RuO₂ Thin Films

Studied the influence of Ar:O₂ gas ratio on the stoichiometry, morphology, crystallinity, and electrochemical performance of sputtered RuO₂ thin films.

Web-Based XPS Analysis Tool

Developed a Python-based web application for XPS analysis, including region selection, background correction, charge correction, and peak fitting workflows.
